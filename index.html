Swim Stats
iOS and Android Mobile App

A fast, simple, and clean mobile app for viewing the race times of any of the 400,000-plus swimmers within USA Swimming. Built with Google's Flutter Framework.

I built this app in the summer between my sophomore and junior years of high school.

Tech Stack
  • Flutter Mobile App (Client)
  • Custom Java Backend on AWS getting times from USA Swimming website
What I Learned
  • I attained a solid grasp of the fundamentals and some advanced features of the Flutter Framework, for example:
    • Simple Custom Animations with an Animation Controller
    • Managing routing with the fluro router package
    • Outsourcing logic to separate classes
    • Saving data to the disk with the path_provider package

Meteor Storm
iOS and Android Mobile Game

Meteor Storm is a mobile game played by saving the Earth from meteors flying towards it from the edges of the screen. The game keeps track of the user's high score, allowing players to compare their skills and compete for the best score.

I built Meteor Storm over about 3 months during January - March of my freshman year of high school. The game was built with the Corona Game Engine.

Tech Stack
  • Corona Game Engine with the Lua Programming Language
What I Learned
  • I learned a ton about game programming
  • I gained a strong understanding of how large pieces of software are built
  • Gained experience in built complex software

2017

Bouncing Balls
Android Mobile App

A fun mobile game I built in eigth grade with MIT App Inventor. I was the first student at my middle school to have both built and published a mobile app. The game I built is played by tapping the grey balls floating around the screen. However, there are also orange balls floating around the screen! Points are received by tapping the grey balls and the game ends when any orange ball is accidentally tapped. The grey balls always travel under their orange counterparts when they collide, making the game challenging to master.

The user's highest score is recorded and saved to the device to motivate the player to continually get better at the game.

Tech Stack
  • Android mobile app
What I Learned
  • I gained a solid grasp of core programming concepts and how to apply them to building a mobile app

About Me

My Journey

Scratch
In the summer of 2016, two friends of mine introduced me to Scratch. For reference, Scratch is a website that allows users to build interactive programs, animations, and games with a code made up of draggable blocks..

I loved Scratch. For the next six months, all I wanted to do in my free time was build things with Scratch. Through the time I spent with Scratch, I developed an strong understanding of fundamental programming concepts, and soon I began to reach further.

Bouncing Balls
Over the first few months of 2017, I built a small game called Bouncing Balls with MIT App Inventor, using a block-programming system similar to but slightly more advanced than Scratch.

Web Dev
In Spring of 2017, I taught myself basic web development (HTML, CSS, JS, and jQuery), which really kicked off my journey into development. I built a few small sites with Bootstrap that spring, but by the next fall, I had grown an interest in making games.

Meteor Storm
In December 2017, during my freshman year of high school, I began my first real software project. I build a mobile game with the Corona Game engine. I learned Lua and the Corona Game Engine's API with a Udemy Course, and after completing it, I was off to the races building my own game! Over the next three months, I built Meteor Storm, a relatively simple game where the user taps and destroys asteroids flying towards the Earth in the center of the screen. If enough meteors hit the Earth, the Earth explodes and the game ends.

Backend Web Dev
Over the summer of 2018, I learned backend web development with Node.js, Express, MongoDB, and the ejs templating engine, primarily from Colt Steele's Udemy Course During the following fall semester, I slowly built CodeJournal, a blogging platform that I hoped would allow people like me to share what they were learning and read about what others were learning as well. Unfortunately, I didn't have a clear vision for the project, and it was never totally finished. I hope to come back one day, give it some shine, and turn it into the site I should've built it into all along.

Mobile Dev with Flutter
In the Spring of 2019, I began learning Flutter because of its cross-platform nature. I wanted to build mobile apps, and Flutter seemed to be fastest way to do that.

Finished.
After spending around 3 months learning the key parts of the Flutter Framework, I began working on a Finished. I wanted to build an app that allowed me to mark exactlywhen I was going to complete an assignment. That year when I was swimming for both a club team and my high school team, which left me with very little time to complete homework for my stacked courseload.

I wanted to give myself the peace of mind of knowing I was going to be able finish everything. Finished. was born from that desire. It features a built-in calendar that makes it easy to mark exactly when you planned to get something done before it was due. Since, its release, I've received thanks from countless parents and students who've used Finished. to help them manage their own busy schedules.

Swim Stats
Over the summer of 2019, I also built Swim Stats, a mobile app that allows users to look up the times of any of the 400,000 swimmers with USA Swimming. Since its release, the app has seen consistent accelerating growth as it spreads throughout the swimming community.

Now and Beyond
I hope to expand the reach Finished. and Swim Stats as I continue to work on them. I'm excited to see what 2020 brings.
